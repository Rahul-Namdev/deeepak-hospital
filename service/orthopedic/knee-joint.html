KNEE JOINT REPLACEMENT

Knee Joint Replacement and Treatment Options

Knee joint replacement, also known as knee arthroplasty, is a common surgical procedure designed to relieve pain and restore function in severely diseased knee joints. This procedure is often necessary when the knee joint is damaged by arthritis or injury, making daily activities painful and difficult.

What is Knee Joint Replacement?

Knee joint replacement involves removing damaged cartilage and bone from the surface of the knee joint and replacing them with artificial components. These components are typically made of metal alloys, high-grade plastics, and polymers. The aim is to replicate the function of a healthy knee and provide pain relief, improved mobility, and a better quality of life.

  • Partial Knee Replacement: Involves replacing only the most damaged part of the knee while preserving healthy bone and ligaments.
  • Total Knee Replacement: This procedure replaces the entire knee joint and is considered when all other treatments have failed to provide relief.
