ARTHROSCOPY AND KEYHOLE SURGERIES

Arthroscopy and keyhole surgeries are minimally invasive surgical techniques used to diagnose and treat joint-related issues with precision and minimal disruption to surrounding tissues. At Deepak Hospital, Dr. Bhaskar Shetty and Dr. Anvith Shetty specialized in providing Keyhole Surgeries and minimal invasive surgeries.

What is Arthroscopy?

Arthroscopy is a procedure used to diagnose and treat problems inside a joint. It involves making small incisions through which a tiny camera, known as an arthroscope, is inserted into the joint. This allows the surgeon to view inside the joint on a screen and perform necessary treatments using specialized instruments.

Benefits of Arthroscopy
  • Minimally Invasive: Small incisions result in less tissue damage compared to traditional open surgery.
  • Reduced Pain and Scarring: Patients typically experience less postoperative pain and minimal scarring.
  • Faster Recovery: Due to the minimally invasive nature, recovery times are shorter, allowing patients to return to daily activities more quickly.
  • Accurate Diagnosis: The camera provides a clear view of the joint, aiding in accurate diagnosis and treatment.
Common Conditions Treated with Arthroscopy
  • Torn Cartilage or Meniscus: Repair or removal of damaged cartilage in the knee or other joints.
  • Ligament Repairs: Such as ACL reconstruction in the knee.
  • Shoulder Impingement: Removal of inflamed tissue or bone spurs.
  • joint Infections: Clearing out infections within the joint
  • Loose Bodies: Removal of loose bone or cartilage fragments.
Keyhole Surgery

Keyhole surgery, similar to arthroscopy, involves using small incisions and specialized instruments to perform surgical procedures. It is commonly used for a variety of surgeries, including gallbladder removal, appendix removal, and gynecological procedures.

Treatment and Recovery Process
  1. Consultation and Diagnosis: An initial consultation with Dr. Bhaskar Shetty and Dr, Anvith Shetty will /include a thorough examination, medical history review, and diagnostic tests, such as MRI or X-rays, blood investigation to assess the Pathology.
  2. Surgical Procedure: The arthroscopic or keyhole surgery is performed under Spinal or general depending on the case. The procedure takes about 1 to 2 hours
  3. Postoperative Care: After the surgery, patients are monitored in the recovery area. Instructions for care at home, including wound care, medications, and physical therapy, are provided to ensure a smooth recovery.
  4. Rehabilitation: Physical therapy is often recommended to restore joint function and strengthen surrounding muscles. Dr. Shetty’s and his team will create a personalized rehabilitation plan tailored to each patient’s needs.
