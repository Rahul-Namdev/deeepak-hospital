JOINT PRESERVATION SURGERY

Joint preservation surgery is an innovative approach to managing joint conditions that aims to delay or avoid the need for joint replacement. At Deepak Hospital, Dr. Bhaskar Shetty , Dr Anvith Shetty and their team specialize in joint preservation techniques designed to maintain natural joint function and improve quality of life for patients and to Avoid knee joint replacement.

What is Joint Preservation Surgery?

Joint preservation surgery focuses on restoring and maintaining the function of a joint rather than replacing it. These procedures are typically used to treat conditions such as early-stage arthritis, cartilage damage, and joint instability, with the goal of preserving the patient's natural joint for as long as possible.

Types of Joint Preservation Techniques
  1. Arthroscopy: A minimally invasive procedure where a small camera / Scope and instruments are used to diagnose and treat joint problems. It can be used to remove loose bodies, repair torn cartilage (Meniscal Repair) to Reconstruct the torn lagament, (ACL, PCL, MCL) and clean the joint.
  2. Osteotomy: Surgical realignment of bones to relieve pressure on the joint, and to prevent damage the cartilage often used for conditions like early-stage arthritis in the knee, where it can delay the need for joint replacement.
  3. Cartilage Restoration: Techniques such as microfracture, autologous chondrocyte implantation (ACI), and osteochondral grafting are used to repair damaged cartilage and promote the growth of healthy cartilage tissue.
  4. Biologic Therapies: Utilizing treatments such as platelet-rich plasma (PRP) and stem cell therapy to promote healing and reduce inflammation within the joint.
Benefits of Joint Preservation Surgery
  • Pain Relief: Reduces pain associated with joint damage or degeneration.
  • Improved Mobility: Improved Joint Function: Restores stability and mobility, allowing patients to return to normal activities.
  • Delay in Joint Replacement: Helps postpone the need for joint replacement surgery, preserving the natural joint for as long as possible.
  • Minimally Invasive: Many procedures are minimally invasive, leading to quicker recovery times and less postoperative discomfort.
