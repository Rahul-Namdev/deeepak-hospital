KIDNEY STONE SURGERY

Kidney stones are a common and often painful condition caused by the formation of mineral deposits in the kidneys. At Deepak Hospital, the expert team of Doctors a range of advanced treatment options is available to manage and resolve kidney stones effectively.

Understanding Kidney Stones

Overview: Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals and salts that forms Stone in the kidneys and bladder. They can vary in size and may cause severe pain as they pass through the ureter. Common symptoms /include intense pain in the back side, blood in the urine, frequent urination, and nausea.

Treatment Options for Kidney Stones

1. Non-Surgical Treatments

  • Medications: Pain relievers and medications may be prescribed to manage discomfort and facilitate the passage of smaller stones. Some medications can help dissolve certain types of stones or prevent new stones from forming.
  • Hydration: Drinking plenty of fluids helps flush out small stones and reduces the risk of future stones.
  • Dietary Changes: Modifying dietary habits to reduce intake of substances that contribute to stone formation, such as calcium, oxalates, and sodium, can help manage and prevent kidney stones.

2.Surgical Treatments

  • Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy (ESWL): A non-invasive procedure where shock waves are directed at the kidney stones from outside the body. The shock waves break the stones into smaller fragments that can be passed more easily through the urinary tract..
  • Ureteroscopy: A minimally invasive procedure where a thin tube with a camera (ureteroscope) is inserted through the urethra and bladder to reach the ureter and kidney. Stones are either removed or broken into smaller pieces using laser technology for easier passage.
  • Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL): Used for larger stones or stones in a difficult location. A small incision is made in the back, and a nephroscope is inserted to remove or break up the stones directly. This procedure is performed under general anesthesia.
  • Open Surgery: Rarely needed, this approach involves a larger incision to remove stones and is generally reserved for very large stones or complicated cases where other methods are not suitable.
Advantages of Modern Treatment Techniquesn
  1. Minimally Invasive: Many procedures are performed with small incisions or no incisions, leading to less pain and quicker recovery.
  2. Effective Pain Management: Advanced techniques and medications effectively manage pain and discomfort during and after the procedure.
  3. Quick Recovery: Patients often experience shorter hospital stays and faster return to normal activities with modern surgical methods.
Postoperative Care and Recovery
  1. Initial Care:Follow postoperative instructions for wound care, medication, and activity restrictions to ensure a smooth recovery.
  2. Follow-Up Visits: Regular check-ups with expeart team of Doctors to monitor healing, evaluate the success of the treatment, and address any concerns.
  3. Lifestyle and Dietary Adjustments: Adhering to dietary recommendations and maintaining hydration can help prevent future kidney stones.
